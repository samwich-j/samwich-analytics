# LinkedIn Post Draft

New blog post is live.

This one looks at demand forecasting for D2C brands and why one model will never be enough. I built a multi-channel inventory forecasting dashboard based on @Nomatic (I'm a big fan of their backpacks, keep up the great work!) using Prophet for the D2C side and Monte Carlo simulation for the B2B wholesale side. The core problem is that these two channels are fundamentally different statistical problems. D2C sales are smooth and seasonal, but wholesale is mostly zeros with sporadic large orders that can swing by thousands of units.

The B2B side is where I learned the most. Intermittent demand turned out to be a whole problem class I didn't know existed, with methods like Croston's, TSB, and log-normal distribution fitting that I had to pick up along the way. The post also covers what the industry is doing now with virtual pooling, ML integration, and real-time recalibration. Full post with sources and dashboard on samwich-analytics.com.

For anyone working in e-commerce, supply chain, or inventory planning, how does your team handle forecasting across channels with very different demand patterns?
