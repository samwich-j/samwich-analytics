# LinkedIn + Blog Content Strategy

## Overview

**Goal:** Build a visible, professional presence that demonstrates analytical thinking — not just dashboards, but the reasoning behind them.

**Format:** Each topic gets a blog post on samwich-analytics.com with a LinkedIn promotion cycle.

**Cadence:** One topic per week (or every two weeks if research is heavy).

**Posting schedule per topic:**

* **Thu/Fri:** Blog post goes live + one LinkedIn summary post with link in body and pinned first comment. Attach dashboard PDF if applicable. Screenshots of dashboard inline in the blog post.

**Posting time:** Thursday or Friday, 8:00–10:00 AM

\---

## Style Rules

* **No "LinkedIn Broetry."** No one-sentence dramatic paragraphs. Write in standard 2-3 sentence paragraphs.
* **Objective tone.** Grounded, analytical, professional. No fake vulnerability or personal life lessons.
* **No emojis** (or at most 2-3 standard bullet symbols for lists).
* **Lead with the problem, not the tool.** "Why do students drop out?" is more compelling than "I built a classification model."
* **End each LinkedIn post with one direct question** aimed at industry professionals to invite engagement.
* **Connect to GT OMSA journey** where natural (incoming Fall 2026 cohort), but don't force it.

## Content Calendar

### Week 1: Student Retention Prediction (Higher Education / UVU)

* **Problem:** Freshman dropout and "summer melt" — students who enroll but never show up or leave within the first year. Especially acute at open-enrollment institutions with non-traditional demographics.
* **Approach:** Review current methods — classification models using demographics (bias concerns), LMS/Canvas activity tracking (reactive, not predictive), surveys (low response, voluntary). Discuss what a better early-warning system would look like.
* **Dashboard:** None (proprietary data). This is a thought piece.
* **Why first:** Directly tied to current role at UVU. Establishes domain expertise and critical thinking. Sets the tone that the blog is about ideas, not just visuals.

### Week 2: Avoidable ED Burden \& Healthcare Access (Healthcare / Intermountain Health)

* **Problem:** Preventable emergency department visits driven by lack of primary care access, SDOH factors, and geographic gaps in community health infrastructure.
* **Approach:** ZCTA-level risk scoring, SDOH driver analysis, facility deployment framework for prioritizing community health interventions.
* **Dashboard:** Yes — IH Power BI dashboard (2-page, already built).
* **Why second:** Natural follow-up to the healthcare angle. Dashboard is ready to showcase.

### Week 3: Revenue Growth Management in Beverage Distribution (CPG / Swire Coca-Cola)

* **Problem:** When revenue declines year-over-year, how do you diagnose whether it's a pricing problem, a volume problem, or a product mix problem? And are your promotions actually driving revenue or destroying value?
* **Approach:** Revenue decomposition (price × volume × mix), promotional ROI analysis, price elasticity modeling, Monte Carlo pricing simulation.
* **Dashboard:** Yes — Swire RGM Power BI dashboard (already built).
* **Why third:** Strongest technical project. By week 3, the blog has momentum and this is the showpiece.

### Week 4: Multi-Channel Demand Forecasting (E-commerce/DTC / Nomatic)

* **Problem:** Rising customer acquisition costs and unpredictable demand across direct-to-consumer channels. How do you forecast demand and allocate marketing spend when you sell through your own site, Amazon, and retail simultaneously?
* **Approach:** Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) modeling, time-series forecasting across channels, Market Mix Modeling for attribution.
* **Dashboard:** Yes — depends on Nomatic project status.

### Week 5: Player Engagement \& Churn in Gaming (Tech / Halo Studios)

* **Problem:** Multiplayer player churn and engagement decay. How do you identify at-risk players before they leave and optimize matchmaking to keep sessions fun?
* **Approach:** Telemetry analysis, churn prediction models, Bayesian matchmaking concepts.
* **Dashboard:** TBD — depends on Halo project status.
